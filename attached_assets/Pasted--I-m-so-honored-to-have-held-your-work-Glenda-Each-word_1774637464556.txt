Below is my draft endorsement. I’m happy to make any edits you would like.

What happens when we write ourselves whole? What happens when we give ourselves permission to re-remember our ancestries, our fantasies, and face the fabrications that have fractured parts of ourselves?

I experienced Glenda’s work as inwardly healing. She takes us on a journey of restoration. She does not tell us how to reclaim our lives; she shows it being done.

The unlearning.
The unwinding.
The searching.
The truth-finding.
The light upon light.
The discoveries.
The ocean dives that transform us into mermaids.
The hearing that teaches wholeness.
The healing that is holy.

There is beauty in a love that is rough around the edges. A translanguaging that layers meaning into a profoundly magical tapestry of being. This is a book of embodied courage. A blueprint to becoming. An art that is ceremonial. Each invocation an invitation to emerge sacred, scarred, and no longer scared. A hope and a prayer. Dream research. Glenda shares, “I likened the experience to an internal guided meditation, but on a much more visceral level. I swam within my own body, identifying and tending to areas of emotional grief or physical hurt. This process was not just metaphorical; I felt as if I was physically present within my own being, addressing areas that were eroded or depleted. While I could not precisely pinpoint these locations, I intuitively sensed I was nurturing and restoring my inner self.”

Perhaps the quiet answer this book leaves us with is, yes. Yes, we can release what no longer aligns. Yes, we can untether ourselves from the illusion of security tied to the versions of ourselves we have outgrown. This, my friends, is the salt that saves us.

Sending love and gratitude,

Vajra
